What causes migratory stress?

Migration as a series of losses and changes

Migratory stress, also known as migratory grief or mourning, is a condition that occurs when the changes brought on by migration cannot be overcome. This may refer to changes that occur before, during, and after migrating, including any reoccurring medical issues and underlying social circumstances.

It is a distress that affects both the migrant and any loved ones they may have left behind.

Migratory mourning is a stress of multiple causes and reasons that includes both the grieving of what was lost paired with the effort to adapt to this new change. Too much stress can become problematic when it occurs to the point that it interferes with our ability to fully adapt to the new change.

What causes stress?

Stress is a natural response that our body develops when we are forced to adapt to new situations such as a move to a new location or the learning of a new language.
